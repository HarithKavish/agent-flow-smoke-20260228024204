What Is Marine Bioluminescence?

Bioluminescence is the production and emission of light by living organisms. In marine ecosystems, this phenomenon is particularly striking, with countless species capable of generating light through a chemical reaction. This light can range from faint glow to intense flashes, serving various ecological functions.

This process involves a chemical reaction between two molecules: luciferin and luciferase. When oxygen is present, luciferase catalyzes the oxidation of luciferin, producing light. The wavelength and intensity of the emitted light can vary widely among species, often adapted to their specific needs.

How Bioluminescence Works

Chemical Basis

The core of bioluminescence lies in the luciferin-luciferase system. Luciferin is a molecule that, when combined with oxygen and energy (often from ATP), is converted by luciferase into light. The exact chemical pathways differ among species, but the principle remains consistent.
